Synopsis:


The Undiscovered Self is a prescient call to consciousness made by famed swiss psychotherapist Carl Jung. Carl Jung describes modern mans precarious situation in which we have conquered science and technology through the atom bomb yet have not acquired the necessary wisdom to have control of mass extinction. Through the sobering recounting of Societies pitfalls, Jung aims to point the reader in the only direction he feels will save the future of humanity, he pushes the individual back into themselves. Jung believed the only way to conquer our animalistic and dangerous tendencies is to reach back into our unconscious and transform our irrational and base nature through the discovery of ourselves. Bringing the unconscious into the conscious mind. Jung describes how we as a species may move forward in illuminating the dark aspects of human nature and bringing them into understanding. This process Jung described was the role of Christianity within Western civilization. Jung gives examples of how one can discover the undiscovered self through self transformation and the exploration of myth, history and tradition.


Favourite Quote:


"This mood of destruction makes itself felt everywhere, politically, socially and philosophically. We are living in what the greeks called the καιρός-the right time-for a 'metamorphosis of the Gods' i.e., of the fundamental principles and symbols. This peculiarity of our time, which is certainly not of our conscious choosing, is the expression of the unconscious man within us who is changing. Coming generations will have to take account of this momentous transformation if humanity is not to destroy itself through the might of its own technology and science."
